# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-02-07*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Kowalah is an enterprise AI impact partner that delivers rapid business value through AI Impact Sprints, then scales transformation across your organization.

**What it does:** Kowalah embeds with your key stakeholders to uncover the 2-3 AI use cases tied to strategic goals, builds working solutions (custom GPTs, Claude skills, AI agents) in weeks, then uses those proof points to drive organization-wide change enablement, training, and ongoing expert delivery. The Kowalah platform gives clients intelligent visibility across their entire AI program, with conversational AI that understands the full context of their projects, initiatives, and use cases, alongside ongoing expert delivery.

**Product category:** Enterprise AI Impact Partner (outcome-focused, implies ongoing relationship, not one-time engagement)

**Product type:** Services-plus-platform (hands-on implementation services + AI program management platform + ongoing expert delivery subscription)

**Platform capabilities (current):**
- Conversational Digital CAIO with full context of the client's AI program (projects, initiatives, use cases, risks, decisions)
- Project and initiative management with real-time status tracking (idea → validated → in development → deployed)
- Expert Request submission and tracking with quota management
- Accelerator library (pre-built AI workflow templates by industry and function)
- Dual ownership model on every project (client owner + Kowalah owner), making the partnership visible in software
- AI Ambassadors program visibility, everyone can see what's happening across the organization

**AI-native delivery model:**
Kowalah uses its own AI agents internally to deliver client work (project management, risk monitoring, status reporting). This means clients experience AI-native professional services as the delivery mechanism itself. They don't have to imagine what AI-native operations look like, they experience it through how Kowalah runs their engagement. This is a capability that traditional consultancies and training companies cannot replicate. Currently internal-only (not client-facing), with plans to extend client-facing agent capabilities over time.

**Business model:**
- AI Impact Sprint: Fixed fee (£6,000-40,000) for rapid value delivery
- Change Enablement: Per-employee pricing (from £117/employee) for organization-wide transformation
- Managed Service (Kowalah Essential): £12,500/month with 12 Expert Requests/quarter
- Digital CAIO Platform: Free tier (10 messages), Individual (£59/mo), Digital (£2,000/mo), Essential (£12,500/mo), Executive (£25,000/mo), Enterprise (£50,000+/mo)

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Mid-sized organizations with 1,000-10,000 employees, significant revenue (£40M-£800M), outside the technology sector. Industries: manufacturing, finance, healthcare, professional services, retail. They have budget for AI initiatives but lack internal AI leadership expertise.

**Decision-makers:** CEO/Managing Partner (primary), CIO/IT Director (secondary), CHRO/Head of People (supporting), CFO/Finance Director (supporting), Business Unit Leaders (supporting)

**Primary use case:** Organization needs AI leadership and rapid business value but can't justify or find qualified CAIO talent, and doesn't want a 12-week strategy engagement that delivers a deck instead of results.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Deliver measurable AI business value tied to strategic goals (board-ready proof points)
- Build internal AI capability and champions who drive adoption organically
- Scale from initial wins to organization-wide AI transformation without losing momentum
- Keep ideas flowing into production (prevent the "Enthusiasm Gap" where post-training excitement dies in IT backlogs)

**Best-fit customer signals:**
- Knowledge work dominant with siloed, inefficient processes
- Already attempted AI rollout but not getting value (Copilot/Gemini deployed, low adoption)
- Everything becomes an IT project (simple ideas die in technical backlogs)
- Limited IT and L&D capacity relative to size
- CEO/senior leader as champion with transformation mindset (views AI as business change, not IT project)

**Use cases:**
- AI Impact Sprint: Embed with key stakeholders, identify strategic use cases, build working solutions in weeks
- Change Enablement: Scale adoption using Sprint proof points as internal case studies
- AI Training: Progressive AI 101/102/201 built around real internal examples
- Expert Requests: Ongoing custom GPT, Claude skill, and AI agent development on demand
- AI Program Management: Track projects, initiatives, and use cases across the organization with intelligent visibility
- AI Advisory Platform: 24/7 strategic AI guidance with full context of the client's AI program (Digital CAIO)

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Organizations know AI matters but are stuck between shadow AI chaos and failed corporate deployments. The big consultancies sell 12-week strategy engagements that deliver decks, not results. Meanwhile, employees are already using AI in secret with no governance.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Human CAIO hiring: 6-12 months to recruit, £250k-400k+ salary, single person's experience, turnover risk
- Big consultancies (Deloitte, Accenture, McKinsey): Governance-first approach, months of discovery, strategy decks that gather dust, no working solutions delivered
- AI training companies: Generic tutorials with no link to strategic goals, theoretical not practical
- DIY approach: No expertise to evaluate vendors, build solutions, or drive adoption at scale

**What it costs them:** Failed AI pilots that erode board confidence, shadow AI creating security risks, competitors pulling ahead, employees frustrated with clunky corporate tools while using better free ones in secret, wasted budget on tools with low adoption.

**Emotional tension:** Board pressure to "do something with AI" creates urgency. Fear of making costly mistakes creates paralysis. Seeing competitors move faster creates anxiety. The gap between AI hype and practical reality creates confusion.

## Competitive Landscape

**Alternative Approach 1 - Internal DIY / Technical Rollout:** "Buy tool, let IT handle it." Buy ChatGPT/Copilot/Gemini, have internal IT deploy, ad-hoc training by HR/L&D. Falls short because IT treats it as a technical project, not a business transformation. No change enablement. Ideas die in IT backlog.

**Alternative Approach 2 - Large Consultancy / GSI:** Deloitte, Accenture, PwC, McKinsey AI practices. Focus on building custom models, technical integration, governance frameworks. Falls short because heavy implementation, light change enablement. 12-week discovery phases. Strategy decks at premium rates. No working solutions. Project-based, not partnership.

**Alternative Approach 3 - Training-First Solutions:** Independent AI consultants, AI training platforms, L&D companies adding AI modules. Falls short because training creates excitement but not execution (the Enthusiasm Gap). Generic tutorials with no link to strategic goals. No mechanism to turn ideas into production solutions.

**Also competing with:** In-house CAIO hiring. 6-12 months to recruit, £250k-400k+ salary, single person's experience vs. collective intelligence, turnover risk.

**Platform gap across all alternatives:** No competitor provides a platform that gives clients intelligent, conversational visibility across their AI program. Consultancies send slide decks. Training companies have no delivery mechanism. DIY approaches lack structured tracking. Kowalah's platform turns program management into a living, AI-powered system that clients interact with daily.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- Sprint-first methodology: Working AI solutions delivered in weeks, not a strategy deck after months
- Embedded approach: We get into the weeds with your key stakeholders and build alongside them ("Bring business to AI", not "AI to business")
- Proof points before scale: Board-ready results before asking for full program commitment
- Prevents the Enthusiasm Gap: Expert Requests turn ideas into production solutions quickly, so post-training excitement doesn't die in IT backlogs
- Ground Game vs Big Bang: We systematically move each person one step forward from wherever they are (AI-negative, amateur users, power users), not company-wide training and hope
- Multi-platform expertise: Deep knowledge of Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini, recommending the right tool for each use case
- Services + platform: Ongoing Expert Requests keep value compounding, not a one-off project
- Internal champions: Your people build solutions with us, creating advocates who drive adoption through first-hand experience
- AI-native delivery: Kowalah uses AI agents in its own delivery process (project management, risk monitoring, reporting), so clients experience AI-native professional services firsthand, teaching by doing, not just telling
- Program visibility: The Kowalah platform gives clients intelligent, conversational visibility across all their AI projects, initiatives, and use cases, not just a dashboard but an AI that understands the full context

**How we do it differently:** We don't start with training, we start by embedding with your people, understanding their real work, and building AI solutions that deliver business value. Then we scale that across your organization. "Here's what Sarah in Finance built" is more powerful than any generic AI tutorial.

**Why that's better:** Rapid proof of value de-risks the full program. Internal champions drive adoption more credibly than external trainers. Ongoing Expert Requests mean ideas don't die after the initial engagement. The flipped sequence (build first, train second) prevents the Enthusiasm Gap that kills most AI initiatives. The platform gives clients real-time visibility across their entire AI program, and because Kowalah delivers AI-natively, clients experience what modern AI operations look like through how their own engagement is run.

**Why customers choose us:** Speed to value, specificity of solutions (custom GPTs, Claude skills, agents tied to their actual work), the ongoing delivery model that keeps innovation flowing, and a platform that gives them intelligent visibility across their entire AI program.
